Family Succession Planning and Estate Management

November 15, 2025 5 min read By Sarah Kimani

Proper succession planning ensures your family's financial security and minimizes disputes. This article covers wills, trusts, inheritance laws in Kenya, and the importance of early estate planning. We explain how to protect your assets and ensure your wishes are carried out according to the law.

Thinking about what happens after you are gone is never easy, but it is one of the most important things you can do for your loved ones. In Kenya, the Law of Succession Act provides a legal framework for the administration of estates, but without a clear plan, the process can become a source of conflict and stress for your family.

The Importance of a Will

A will is a legal document that expresses your wishes for how your property should be distributed after your death. Dying without a valid will (intestate) means your assets will be distributed according to a fixed formula set by the law, which may not align with your wishes.

Key benefits of having a will include:

  • Control over Asset Distribution: You decide who inherits your property and in what proportions.
  • Appointing an Executor: You can choose a trusted person or institution to manage your estate.
  • Appointing Guardians: You can appoint guardians to care for your minor children.
  • Minimizing Disputes: A clear will can significantly reduce the likelihood of family disputes over your estate.
"Succession planning is a gift to your family. It provides clarity and peace of mind during a difficult time."

Understanding Trusts

A trust is another powerful tool for estate planning. It involves transferring your assets to a trustee, who manages them on behalf of your chosen beneficiaries. Trusts can be set up during your lifetime (living trusts) or created through your will (testamentary trusts).

Trusts offer several advantages, including:

  • Asset Protection: Assets held in a trust are protected from creditors and legal claims against beneficiaries.
  • Professional Management: Trustees can provide professional management of complex assets like businesses or investment portfolios.
  • Flexibility and Control: You can set specific conditions for how and when beneficiaries receive their inheritance.
